LOCK DOWN

From living like a free bird in the remote world to living like a bird in cage, our lives had experienced an unexpected twist and turns which no one could ever dream of, this pandemic had become a nightmare for everyone. Still mid-march everything seemed normal and calm. Then came a curve of pandemic due to this corona virus which made the world upside down. It was unexpected on 20th march our college announced 15 days holidays due to this pandemic, and we were all in cloud nine in the thought of going home so we did pack our bags and returned home with strange journey then before as it was covid times, taking certain precautions such as using face masks, social distancing and sanitisers so on. Then started this worldwide lockdown starting from May 25th and still going on it had been six months of being captured between the four walls of my room. These was for the first time we heard different words like quarantine, SARS…. etc. It was never expected longest vacation one could ever have. Life in lockdown is changing what we do and how we feel. This could be our reality for a while and it’s important that we begin to adapt and accept our new way of life.

Under lockdown circumstances, one cannot go out to work or can go out to malls or cinemas for leisure activities. A lockdown means that people need to stay inside and safe. Essential service like groceries, medications, banks are only allowed to function in this crucial time. Millions of children across the world are facing life in lockdown. This can be a difficult time. Away from friends, away from school, away from education, this generation is missing out on everyday activities we used to take for granted.

Although we go out for necessities there was a haunting fear in minds of people as this corona virus being a contagious disease which spreads through air. Due to this sudden lockdown many people stayed up at home and had quality time together with their families, where as some people could not reach their families and had to stay away till this lockdown ends. There was great loss to the country economically due to cessation of all the activities. Mainly the daily wage workers who migrated to cities or different states leaving their villages suffered a lot due to no income and livelihood, some literally walked back to their home towns as there was no transportation. The homeless people and the shelter less animals were most effected, died in hunger as due to evoking fear of this pandemic and the strict lockdown no body was willing to give some water or food for them.

And nearly huge number of people lost their jobs and some were not paid or paid less then what they get regularly. Mainly the health workers, police officers were the only warriors worked risking their lives to save people lives or help the effected on, so many of them distanced themselves their families to not infect them few of them were biased by neighbourhood people or house owners and some of them lost their lives in order to save others. The graph of our country raised exponentially reaching lakhs of lakhs of corona cases which lead to lack of health facilities or beds in hospital and made the condition vulnerable. The only hope for the people was the discovery of the outbreak for this virus by discovery of vaccine. Scientists have been working on it but it had been a long wait so people are tired on it and started adjusting with these new conditions we are within. I think the only positive thing happened in this lockdown is that the mother earth is being healing due to no pollution due to transportation and cessation of all the activities, that’s why we had a plentiful rainfall rate this year and the animals and birds were having a lively environment to live, in some areas we could see peacocks coming on to roads.

And the main cause is mental health is being affected due to the economic crisis, stress, and more exposure to blue light or screen is also harmful as we can see from small kids, children, adults everyone are dependent on these for entertainment, education (online classes), or jobs (due to work from home) which may literally affect our health and well-being. I wish we could get out this virtual world to our past olden days as soon as possible into the real world.

Priyanka Vallipalli
